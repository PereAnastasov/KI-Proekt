Macedonia Opposition Block Roads Over Electricity Price Hike

The main opposition VMRO DPMNE party on Friday staged a road blockade in central Skopje in protest against the new government led by Zoran Zaev, blaming it for the recent rise in the price of electricity.

Protesters against a rise in electricity prices blocked roads in Skopje on Friday, parking vehicles on Macedonia street in front of the offices of the energy regulatory commission, which determines the price of electricity, and blocking traffic on the adjacent Sts Cyril and Methodius street. The cars displayed banners saying: “The government is comprised of thieves”, “Rich government, poor people”, and “Down with the thieves!”

Opposition VMRO DPMNE party head Hristijan Mickoski launched the protests on Wednesday, announcing a “hot political autumn” with more protests to follow. The party holds the regulatory commission responsible, insisting it is a pawn of the newly formed government led by Social Democrat chief Zoran Zaev.

The opposition sent three demands to the government: to bring back the old electricity bills; introduce nationwide weekends where shoppers won’t have to pay VAT; and professionalization of the energy regulatory commission, which they say is dominated by political figures.

“If they respond positively, we are willing to reconsider our actions, but knowing them, we will see each other again on the streets,” VMRO DPMNE vice president Vlado Misajlovski told the media and the protesters.

The opposition took to the streets just one week after the formation of the new government made up of old partners – the Social Democrats under Zaev and the ethnic Albanian Democratic Union for Integration, DUI, following July 15 elections in which the Social Democrats won a narrow victory over Mickoski’s VMRO DPMNE.

HWhile the opposition insists the motive for the protests is purely social, they come as Mickoski’s leadership of the right-wing party is being contested by a variety of VMRO DPMNE party veterans demanding his resignation. Some observers said they see the protests as a form of distraction tactic against the calls for him to step down from the party leadership. The veterans, belonging to different party factions, some of which were previously excluded from the party by Mickoski, want an extraordinary party congress held, to topple Mickoski. The party leadership insists there is no need for such a congress.
