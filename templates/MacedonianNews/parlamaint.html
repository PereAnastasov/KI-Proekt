Parliament seeking alternative ways to hold sessions in extraordinary conditions




Parliament is seeking alternative ways to hold sessions in extraordinary circumstances, namely online sessions and voting due to the COVID-19 pandemic since it is being estimated that it might not be subdued for at least two more years.

– Parliament is seeking alternative ways to hold sessions in extraordinary circumstances, namely online sessions and voting due to the COVID-19 pandemic since it is being estimated that it might not be subdued for at least two more years.

At a press briefing, Speaker Talat Xhaferi said that it will take two or three months to regulate the alternative way of organizing sessions. In the meantime, they will take place in the dome hall and the votes will be counted manually.



“As Parliament Speaker, it’s my duty to think about the Assembly’s functionality both in regular and extraordinary circumstances. In my first coordination meetings, I presented the idea of seeking alternative ways. Unfortunately, it was used as a spin to claim that the Speaker is trying to find ways to avoid regular functioning of Parliament so as to make up for the lack of a quorum, and the MPs to work from home. However, that’s not the idea,” stressed Xhaferi.

According to him, there are no provisions regulating how Parliament should function in extraordinary conditions.

“The pandemic has shown us that it is necessary to provide alternative ways to work. If it’s not a pandemic, it could be some other things, like fire, earthquake… In such extraordinary circumstances we should find alternative ways for the institution to function,” said Xhaferi.



Parliament on Monday will hold a session to elect the members of the working bodies and to verify the term of the MP replacing Labor Minister Jagoda Shahpaska.

So far, the government has sent to Parliament bills on elementary and secondary education and the President’s office has sent the five decisions of the head of state declaring state of emergency.
